# Assessment Validity and Fitness for Purpose

The platform is designed around the following claim:

> British English Academy provides a CEFR-benchmarked diagnostic placement result that helps learners and institutions select an appropriate course pathway.

The platform should not claim to be an official Cambridge, Pearson, McGraw Hill or Council of Europe exam. It should also not claim universal acceptance by institutions until recognition agreements and empirical validation studies are completed.

## Evidence model

1. **Purpose definition**: paid placement and learning-path recommendation.
2. **Construct definition**: reading, use of English, vocabulary, and optional writing/speaking extension.
3. **Content relevance**: every item is mapped to CEFR level, skill, difficulty and can-do tag.
4. **Reliability**: adaptive termination uses standard error of measurement.
5. **Fairness**: item review, accessibility review and differential item functioning analysis.
6. **Impact**: reports recommend learning steps rather than only giving a label.
7. **Empirical validation**: pilot data must be collected and analysed before high-stakes certification claims.

## Certificate wording

Recommended wording:

> This certificate verifies a British English Academy platform diagnostic placement result. It is intended to support course placement and English-learning planning. External acceptance depends on the receiving institution.
