# CEFR Validation Review

## Purpose

This document helps the developer/content team check whether each BEA course, module, lesson and assessment is mapped to the correct CEFR band.

## CEFR mapping checks

For every lesson:

- [ ] CEFR level is stated.
- [ ] Communicative outcome is stated.
- [ ] Grammar and vocabulary are appropriate for the level.
- [ ] Task complexity matches the level.
- [ ] Productive task expectations match the level.
- [ ] Rubric descriptors match the level.
- [ ] Assessment task is not too easy or too difficult.
- [ ] Feedback supports progression to the next lesson.

## Internal validation status labels

Use these values:

- `mapped_pending_editorial_review`
- `editorially_reviewed`
- `qa_passed`
- `pilot_ready`
- `pilot_completed`
- `externally_validated`

## Important commercial warning

BEA may describe the content as CEFR-aligned or CEFR-benchmarked after internal mapping. Do not claim that the course or certificate is an official Council of Europe, Cambridge, Pearson, Oxford or government qualification unless formal recognition is obtained.
